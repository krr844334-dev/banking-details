A bank is a regulated financial institution that acts as an intermediary, accepting deposits from individuals and businesses, providing safe storage for money, and lending funds to borrowers. They facilitate payments, money transfers, and investment services while playing a crucial role in economic growth. 
Key Functions of Banks
Accepting Deposits: Safekeeping money in various accounts (savings, current, fixed deposits).
Lending Money: Providing loans (personal, business, mortgage) and credit facilities.
Payment Services: Facilitating cheque, cash, and digital transactions (ATMs, UPI, transfers).
Investments & Advisory: Offering wealth management, insurance, and investment products. 
Types of Banks
Retail Banks: Serve individual customers.
Commercial/Corporate Banks: Focus on business clients.
Investment Banks: Handle complex financial transactions and underwriting.
Central Banks: Regulate the banking system and issue currency (e.g., RBI in India, Federal Reserve in the US). 
Banking System Structure
Safety & Regulation: Banks are heavily regulated, often with government backing to ensure stability, such as deposit insurance.
Digital Transformation: Modern banking heavily relies on technology, including mobile apps and online platforms.
History: Evolved from ancient times, with modern banking growing from 17th-century European institutions. 
In India, the banking sector includes public sector, private sector, foreign, and regional rural banks, focusing on both commercial activities and financial inclusion.
